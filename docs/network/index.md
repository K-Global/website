# Ten hubs. Seven regions. One core.

K Global is built around a **Frankfurt (EDDF)** core and radiates outward to
**ten hubs across seven regions** — a route structure of roughly **848 city
pairs (1,696 legs)** live in the ops platform. It's a genuine intercontinental
web: whichever hub you're based at, the rest of the world is only a leg or two
away.

## How the network hangs together

- **Frankfurt is the heart.** Every region ultimately connects back through
  the European core, so long-haul waves and short-haul feed line up the way
  they do at a real group hub.
- **Regional gateways carry their continents.** Singapore, Tokyo and Hong
  Kong anchor Asia; New York and Los Angeles split the United States coasts;
  São Paulo, Sydney and Lagos hold South America, Oceania and Africa.
- **Abu Dhabi is the bridge.** A deliberate Middle East pivot that stitches
  Europe to Asia, Africa and Oceania on the long over-water legs.
