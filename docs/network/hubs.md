# The ten hubs, by region

K Global spans **seven regions and ten hubs**, each with its own character and
role in the wider web. Frankfurt is home; the rest carry their continents.

| Region | Hub | ICAO | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Europe (core)** | Frankfurt | **EDDF** | Home hub and the heart of the network |
| **Asia** | Singapore | **WSSS** | Southeast Asia gateway |
| **Asia** | Hong Kong | **VHHH** | Cargo gateway |
| **Asia** | Tokyo | **RJTT** | North Asia gateway |
| **North America** | New York | **KJFK** | US East Coast |
| **North America** | Los Angeles | **KLAX** | US West Coast |
| **South America** | São Paulo | **SBGR** | South America gateway |
| **Oceania** | Sydney | **YSSY** | Oceania gateway |
| **Africa** | Lagos | **DNMM** | Africa gateway |
| **Middle East** | Abu Dhabi | **OMAA** | Bridge hub linking the regions |

## Regions at a glance

=== "Europe"

    **Frankfurt (EDDF)** is the core. Every wave in the network is timed to
    connect back through it, so short-haul feed and long-haul departures line
    up the way they do at a real group hub. It's the busiest, most varied
    place to be based — every unit and nearly every fleet type passes through.

=== "Asia"

    Three hubs share the continent. **Singapore (WSSS)** covers Southeast Asia,
    **Tokyo (RJTT)** anchors the north, and **Hong Kong (VHHH)** is the cargo
    gateway — a freighter pilot's home in the region.

=== "North America"

    The United States is split by coast: **New York (KJFK)** in the east and
    **Los Angeles (KLAX)** in the west, feeding transatlantic and transpacific
    long-haul respectively.

=== "The rest of the world"

    **São Paulo (SBGR)** holds South America, **Sydney (YSSY)** Oceania, and
    **Lagos (DNMM)** Africa. Tying them together is **Abu Dhabi (OMAA)**, the
    bridge hub built to stitch Europe to Asia, Africa and Oceania across the
    long over-water legs.

!!! tip "Pick your base, pick your flying"
    Where you're based shapes the kind of flying you'll do — mega-hub variety
    at Frankfurt, long over-water legs from Abu Dhabi or Los Angeles, dense
    freight out of Hong Kong. Explore the [business units](../business-units/index.md)
    to see which fleets fly where.
